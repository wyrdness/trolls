# Sources & References

## Classic Folklore Collections

### Norwegian
- Asbjørnsen, P.C. & Moe, J. (1841-1844). *Norske Folkeeventyr* (*Norwegian Folktales*). Various editions and translations.
  - Classic collection including "Three Billy Goats Gruff" and other troll tales
  - Foundation of Norwegian folklore scholarship

- Dasent, G.W. (Trans.) (1859). *Popular Tales from the Norse*. Edinburgh: Edmonston and Douglas.
  - English translation of Asbjørnsen & Moe
  - Introduced Norwegian troll tales to English-speaking world

### Swedish
- Lagerlöf, S. (1906-1907). *Nils Holgerssons underbara resa genom Sverige* (*The Wonderful Adventures of Nils*).
  - Includes Swedish troll and folklore elements

### Danish
- Various Danish folklore collections featuring *trold* traditions

### Icelandic
- Jón Árnason (1862-1864). *Íslenzkar þjóðsögur og æfintýri* (*Icelandic Folktales and Legends*)
  - Icelandic troll and hidden people traditions

## Academic & Scholarly Works

### Norse Mythology & Folklore
- Lindow, J. (2001). *Norse Mythology: A Guide to the Gods, Heroes, Rituals, and Beliefs*. Oxford University Press. ISBN: 978-0195153828
  - Comprehensive academic treatment of Norse mythology including troll origins

- Kvideland, R. & Sehmsdorf, H.K. (Eds.) (1988). *Scandinavian Folk Belief and Legend*. University of Minnesota Press. ISBN: 978-0816617661
  - Scholarly collection of folk beliefs across Scandinavia

- Simpson, J. (1972). *Icelandic Folktales and Legends*. University of California Press.
  - Academic analysis of Icelandic folklore including trolls

### Cultural Analysis
- Honko, L. (1989). "Folkloristic Theories of Genre." *Studies in Oral Folk Literature*. 
  - Analysis of folklore genre including troll tales

- Hafstein, V.T. (2000). "The Elves' Point of View: Cultural Identity in Contemporary Icelandic Elf-Tradition." *Fabula*, 41(1-2), 87-104.
  - Relevant to Icelandic troll/elf overlap

## Literary Analysis

### Ibsen & Grieg
- Ibsen, H. (1867). *Peer Gynt*. 
  - Play featuring trolls in Hall of the Mountain King
  
- Grieg, E. (1875). *Peer Gynt Suite* (incidental music).
  - "In the Hall of the Mountain King" — iconic troll-themed composition

### Tolkien's Trolls
- Tolkien, J.R.R. (1937). *The Hobbit*.
  - Features trolls directly inspired by Scandinavian folklore

- Shippey, T. (2000). *J.R.R. Tolkien: Author of the Century*. Houghton Mifflin.
  - Analysis of Tolkien's use of Norse sources
